San Francisco — Rain & Fog, Block by Block

Rain in SF is nearly uniform and falls almost entirely Nov–Mar. Grey skies are not — the summer marine layer keeps the western avenues foggy while the Mission, in the fog shadow of Twin Peaks, stays sunny. When SF rain actually falls

Average rainy days per month, downtown San Francisco (NOAA 1991–2020). Summer is effectively dry — July averages a single rainy day.

Definitions. A rainy day = ≥0.01 in (0.3 mm) measurable precipitation (NOAA standard). Sunny days = clear + partly-cloudy days; non-sunny / cloudy days = 365 − sunny (mostly-overcast days). City rain-day & rainfall figures are NOAA/NCEI 1991–2020 normals; state & national rainfall are NOAA area-averages (CA 1896–2023; contiguous U.S. ≈30.2 in).

Neighborhood values are a transparent model, not 41 separate weather stations (the city has only a handful). Each neighborhood's non-sunny days = 86 + 92·(coast-proximity) − 14 (Twin-Peaks fog shadow), scaled so the city mean equals the observed citywide ~116 cloudy / ~71 rainy days. Rain gets only a small coast/elevation gradient — real SF rain barely varies across 7 miles. Boundaries: official DataSF “Analysis Neighborhoods.”
